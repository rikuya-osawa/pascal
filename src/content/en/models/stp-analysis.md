# STP Analysis (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)

## Overview
A strategic framework used to break down the market (**Segmentation**), determine which customers to pursue (**Targeting**), and define the unique value and stance to offer in that space (**Positioning**).

## Evaluation (1–5)
**Versatility**:: 5  
**Immediacy**:: 4  
**Difficulty**:: 2  
**Misuse Risk**:: 3  

### Editorial Comments
**Comment**:: A powerful starting point for any market strategy. However, it is meaningless if it ends as a mere **「desk-side analysis」** without real-world validation.

## The First Question
- "To whom, in what context, and exactly how will we win?"

### Objectives
- Prevent the pitfall of trying to appeal to **「everyone」**.
- Clarify the brand's unique position.

### Pitfall Questions
- "Is the market size large enough?"
- "Are there few competitors?"
- "What are we capable of doing?"

## How to Use (Mini-Steps)
1. **Segmentation**: Break down the market.
   - Criteria: Age, behavior, pain points, etc.
2. **Targeting**: Decide which specific customer segments to focus on.
3. **Positioning**: Define the value proposition and points of differentiation.

## Output Examples
- **Bullet Points:**
  - List of segments
  - Rationale for selection
  - Positioning statement
- **Visuals:**
  - Segment Map
  - 2-axis Positioning Map

## Use Cases
- **Business**: Designing new services or products.
- **Daily Life**: Personal career positioning.
- **Decision Making**: When the target customer persona feels ambiguous.

## Common Misuses
- Ending after merely dividing the segments without choosing one.
- Selecting a target based solely on the company's internal perspective.
- Failing to verbalize the specific **「positioning」**.

## Relationship with Other Models
- **High-level Concept**: Competitive Advantage (Moat).
- **Complementary**: 4P / 4C Marketing Mix.
- **Related**: JTBD (Jobs to be Done).
- **Contrast**: Omni-directional (All-round) Strategy.
